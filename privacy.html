5. Microphone and voice features

One optional case — the breast deep-inspiration breath-hold (DIBH) coaching simulation — lets you coach the patient by voice. When you start that case, the trainer asks for microphone permission and uses your browser's built-in speech recognition (the Web Speech API) to detect short coaching phrases such as "breathe in" or "hold".

  • Depending on your browser, speech recognition may be performed on the browser vendor's servers (for example, Google for Chrome). That processing is governed by your browser vendor's own privacy policy, not ours.
  • We do not record, store, or transmit your audio to our servers, and we do not use the microphone anywhere else in the Service.
  • The microphone is released when you leave the case, close the tab, or switch away from it.
  • Microphone access is entirely optional — you can decline it, and on-screen coaching buttons let the case work without voice.
