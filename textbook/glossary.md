# Appendix B - Glossary & Abbreviations

This appendix collects the high-yield terms and acronyms used throughout the book, each defined in one plain-language sentence for quick registry review.

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| 3D-CRT | Three-dimensional conformal radiotherapy, which shapes static beams to the target using CT-based planning and beam's-eye-view MLC or block shaping. |
| 4D-CT | A CT acquired in respiratory-correlated phases so tumor motion over the breathing cycle can be measured and accounted for in planning. |
| 6DOF | Six degrees of freedom, the full set of correctable couch motions: three translations (Lat/Lng/Vrt) plus three rotations (Pitch/Roll/Yaw). |
| ALARA | "As Low As Reasonably Achievable," the radiation-safety principle of keeping dose to staff, patients, and the public as low as practical. |
| AP/Lateral | The standard orthogonal imaging pair, an anterior-posterior view plus a lateral (side) view, used together to localize in all three dimensions. |
| Adaptive radiotherapy (ART) | Re-planning during a treatment course to account for anatomic changes such as tumor shrinkage or weight loss. |
| BED | Biologically effective dose, a way of comparing different fractionation schemes by their predicted biological impact using the linear-quadratic model. |
| Becquerel (Bq) | The SI unit of radioactivity, equal to one nuclear disintegration per second. |
| Brachytherapy (HDR/LDR) | Treatment with sealed radioactive sources placed in or near the tumor, delivered at a high dose rate (HDR, typically Ir-192) or low dose rate (LDR intracavitary, typically Cs-137). |
| Bremsstrahlung | "Braking radiation," the x-rays produced when fast electrons decelerate near a nucleus, which is how a linac target makes its photon beam. |
| CBCT | Cone-beam CT, a volumetric image acquired on the treatment machine to verify and correct patient setup in 3D before treatment. |
| CTCAE | Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events, the standardized scale used to grade the severity of treatment side effects. |
| Characteristic x-ray | An x-ray emitted at a discrete energy when an outer-shell electron fills an inner-shell vacancy, unique to the target element. |
| Clinical Target Volume (CTV) | The GTV plus a margin for suspected microscopic (subclinical) disease spread. |
| Compton scatter | The interaction in which a photon ejects a loosely bound outer electron and scatters with reduced energy, dominant at the megavoltage (MV) energies used in treatment. |
| Conformity index | A plan-quality metric describing how closely the prescription dose conforms to the target volume. |
| d_max | The depth of maximum dose, where the percentage depth dose reaches 100% and which increases with beam energy. |
| DIBH | Deep inspiration breath-hold, a technique where the patient holds a deep breath to move organs (e.g., the heart) out of the beam during treatment. |
| DRR | Digitally reconstructed radiograph, an x-ray-like image computed from the planning CT and used as the reference for 2D setup matching. |
| DVH | Dose-volume histogram, a graph summarizing how much dose is received by what fraction of a target or organ. |
| Deterministic effect | A radiation effect with a dose threshold below which it does not occur and whose severity increases with dose (e.g., skin erythema, cataracts). |
| Electron range (R90/R50/Rp) | Measures of how deep an electron beam penetrates, including the therapeutic range R90, the half-dose depth R50, and the practical range Rp. |
| EPID | Electronic portal imaging device, the digital detector on a linac used to capture MV portal images for setup verification. |
| EQD2 | Equivalent dose in 2-Gy fractions, a way of expressing a treatment course as the dose that would give the same biological effect if delivered in 2-Gy fractions. |
| Equivalent square | A square field that has the same output and depth-dose characteristics as a given rectangular field, approximated as 2ab/(a+b). |
| Fiducial marker | A small implanted marker (often gold) that shows up clearly on images to help localize a soft-tissue target such as the prostate. |
| Four R's | The classic radiobiology principles governing fractionation: Repair, Repopulation, Redistribution, and Reoxygenation. |
| Fractionation | Dividing the total radiation dose into multiple smaller daily treatments to spare normal tissue while still controlling the tumor. |
| Gray (Gy) | The SI unit of absorbed dose, equal to one joule of energy deposited per kilogram of tissue. |
| Gross Tumor Volume (GTV) | The visible, palpable, or imaged extent of the tumor itself. |
| Half-life | The time required for the activity of a radioactive source to decay to one-half of its original value. |
| Half-value layer (HVL) | The thickness of a specified material needed to reduce a beam's intensity to one-half, used as a measure of beam quality. |
| Homogeneity index | A plan-quality metric describing how uniform the dose is within the target volume. |
| IGRT | Image-guided radiation therapy, using imaging at the machine (e.g., kV, CBCT, portal) to verify and correct patient position before each treatment. |
| IMRT | Intensity-modulated radiotherapy, which varies the beam intensity across each field (via moving MLC leaves) to sculpt dose tightly around the target. |
| Internal Target Volume (ITV) | The CTV plus an internal margin that accounts for target motion, such as breathing. |
| Inverse-square law | The principle that beam intensity falls off with the square of the distance from the source, so D2 = D1 x (d1/d2)^2. |
| Isocenter | The fixed point in space, typically at 100 cm from the source, about which the gantry, collimator, and couch all rotate. |
| kV vs MV | Kilovoltage beams give high soft-tissue contrast for imaging via the photoelectric effect, while megavoltage beams are penetrating and treatment-grade, dominated by Compton scatter. |
| LET | Linear energy transfer, the amount of energy a radiation deposits per unit path length, with high-LET radiation being more biologically damaging. |
| Linear accelerator (linac) | The machine that accelerates electrons to produce high-energy electron or x-ray (photon) treatment beams. |
| Linear-quadratic model | The standard radiobiology model describing cell survival with an alpha (linear) and beta (quadratic) component of dose. |
| MLC | Multileaf collimator, the bank of independently moving leaves that shapes the beam aperture and enables IMRT/VMAT. |
| MV portal imaging | Setup verification images made with the treatment (megavoltage) beam itself, captured on an EPID. |
| Monitor unit (MU) | The unit of beam output a linac is calibrated in, where the dose delivered is set by the number of MU. |
| OER | Oxygen enhancement ratio, comparing the dose needed to produce an effect without oxygen versus with oxygen, since oxygenated cells are more radiosensitive. |
| Organ at risk (OAR) | A normal tissue or structure whose radiation sensitivity may limit the plan and whose dose is therefore constrained. |
| PDD | Percentage depth dose, the dose at a given depth expressed as a percentage of the maximum dose along the central axis. |
| PRV | Planning organ-at-risk volume, an OAR plus a margin to account for setup and motion uncertainty. |
| Pair production | A photon interaction (threshold 1.02 MeV) in which a photon converts into an electron-positron pair near a nucleus. |
| Photoelectric effect | An interaction in which a photon is fully absorbed by ejecting an inner-shell electron, important at low (kV) energies and strongly dependent on atomic number. |
| Pitch/Roll/Yaw | The three rotational degrees of freedom of the couch, correcting tilt around the lateral, longitudinal, and vertical axes respectively. |
| Planning Target Volume (PTV) | The CTV (or ITV) plus a setup margin for daily positioning uncertainty, the volume the plan is actually prescribed to. |
| RBE | Relative biological effectiveness, comparing a test radiation's dose to a reference radiation's dose for the same biological effect. |
| Rad/Rem/Roentgen | Older (conventional) radiation units: the rad for absorbed dose (1 rad = 0.01 Gy), the rem for equivalent dose (1 rem = 0.01 Sv), and the roentgen for exposure in air. |
| SAD | Source-to-axis distance, the distance from the radiation source to the isocenter, typically 100 cm. |
| SBRT/SRS | Stereotactic body radiotherapy and stereotactic radiosurgery, highly precise treatments delivering large doses in a few fractions (SBRT, body) or a single session (SRS, intracranial). |
| SGRT | Surface-guided radiation therapy, using optical surface imaging to position and monitor the patient without added dose. |
| SSD | Source-to-surface distance, the distance from the radiation source to the patient's skin surface. |
| Sievert (Sv) | The SI unit of equivalent (and effective) dose, accounting for the biological harm of different radiation types. |
| Stochastic effect | A radiation effect with no dose threshold, where increasing dose raises the probability (not the severity) of the effect, such as cancer induction. |
| TAR | Tissue-air ratio, the ratio of dose at a point in tissue to the dose at the same point in air, used in older isocentric calculations. |
| TBI | Total body irradiation, the irradiation of the whole body, commonly used as conditioning before a bone marrow transplant. |
| TD5/5 | The tolerance dose expected to cause a severe complication in 5% of patients within 5 years, used to describe normal-tissue tolerance. |
| TMR | Tissue-maximum ratio, the ratio of dose at depth to the dose at the depth of maximum dose, used in isocentric (SAD) calculations. |
| TPR | Tissue-phantom ratio, the ratio of dose at a point to the dose at a fixed reference depth in a phantom, similar to TMR. |
| Thermoplastic mask | A custom heat-molded immobilization device that secures the head or body region in the same position each day. |
| VMAT | Volumetric-modulated arc therapy, a form of IMRT that delivers dose continuously as the gantry rotates in one or more arcs. |
| Wedge | A beam-modifying device (physical or dynamic) that tilts the dose distribution by progressively attenuating one side of the field. |
